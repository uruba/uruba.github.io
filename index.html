Václav's story

Computers have always been my passion. I started learning some basic HTML and Pascal back in the early 2000s and it was just then when I embarked on a great journey of incessantly acquiring exciting knowledge and creating awesome new things for others to use and enjoy.
It is indeed with great fervour and alacrity that I still venture down this path – and continue to adopt new wisdom across various fields on the way. To wit, I do love the process!

In high school I came to contact with Pascal (and Delphi development environment) which I'm very fond of to this day (I still experiment with Free Pascal from time to time). Out of my curiosity I also started to learn HTML + PHP (for the web programming needs) and C# (as a then-very-modern language to help me delve into the contemporary trends). And I definitely cannot forget Java (Standard Edition) – in university I had an extensive course covering programming in this language which I immensely enjoyed.

Although I have always been a big Microsoft fan and admirer (back in 2012 I also made some little apps for Windows Phone 7 and Windows 8 to learn the ropes a little), I recently started with programming applications for Google's Android. That was in 2014, since when my first app's hit the Play Store.

I need not to forget about the web which is quite a big thing nowadays and I certainly do not intend to lag behind :-)

More often than not, the language of choice when creating server-side things for the web is PHP – and that was mine too. Coming from the universe of strongly-typed languages like Pascal, C# and Java I had quite a hard time getting used to its dynamic typing (giving you less control over the things) but that was just a minor hurdle.
Whilst designing and creating new web applications I also came to discover (and subsequently tried to harness :-)) the power of WordPress, a CMS that enabled me to create content-based websites quickly and easily (albeit at the cost of mildly slower website's speed).

Then I also explored the world of Ruby and its flagship framework Ruby on Rails. I had immediately took a liking to the language owing to its simplicity and pure object-oriented (unlike PHP) design, resulting in an easily readable code (yet not as explicit as it is with other languages; which sometimes puts your knowledge of the syntax, as well as your imagination, to a real test). I must say that I'm still a beginner in this realm so I still have a lot to learn. Also, thanks to Rails I've came to contact with a trio of awesome code preprocessors – Slim for HTML, SASS for CSS and finally CoffeeScript for JavaScript.

And because it's always beneficial to broaden one's expertise into other territories, I worked also as a copywriter for three years – mainly in order to gain experience in the vast fields of on-line marketing. During that time, I also got the opportunity to work on some smaller PPC marketing campaigns, webpages' optimization for search engines or even some UI work to accommodate better user experience; so that's what I did. I have to say that I truly enjoy the world that opens before us when seeking for new, uncontested challenges.

Are you interested in my service? Do you want to ask me anything? Feel free to ring me up or send me a message whenever you see fit!
